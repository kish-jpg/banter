# Banter — Product Context

register: product (single brand moment: the logged-out landing/demo)

## Product purpose

AI texting coach. Reads a dating/social conversation the user provides, shows a signal
read (interest, warmth, reciprocity, momentum), hands them 3 replies grounded in cited
communication psychology, grades their own attempts, and remembers each relationship
via consent-clean personas. It teaches skill; the win condition is a real date, not a
longer thread. Sourced from .planning/INTENT-PERSONA-ENGINE.md and the confirmed
founder interview (2026-07-10).

## Users

Mostly 20s-30s dating-app users, texting in the evening on a phone, often lying in bed
with the dating app open in the other hand. Slightly anxious, emotionally invested,
privacy-sensitive (they are pasting intimate conversations). Secondary: friend/business
texters (shallow support v1).

**Scene sentence (theme):** someone in bed at 11pm, phone on dark mode, a conversation
that matters open in the other app, heart rate a little up. Forces: dark, calm, warm,
low-glare, zero clutter.

## Brand and tone

- Wordmark: lowercase "banter." with a coral period. Coral #ff5c7a is the single accent.
- Voice: a warm, direct friend looking over your shoulder. Never robotic, never clinical,
  never bro-y. Lowercase-leaning UI labels.
- Ethics are part of the brand: evidence-based psychology with citations, PUA tactics
  hard-banned, strict data provenance, one-tap delete-everything. The interface must
  FEEL trustworthy: nothing surveillance-y, nothing manipulative, reads are bands not
  clinical percentages.

## Anti-references

- RizzGPT / pickup-line generators: neon, bro-coded, manipulation-adjacent. Never.
- Clinical dashboards: this is not analytics about a human being.
- Duolingo-style mascot cuteness: progression yes, cartoon energy no.
- Generic SaaS dark template: identical gray cards everywhere.

## Strategic principles

- One primary action per screen (Wispr Flow bar). Calm > dense.
- First value before any signup or profile question.
- The magic moment is a reply worth sending, explained. Everything optimizes
  time-to-that or reasons-to-return (threads, progression, personas).
- Dates are the win condition; the UI celebrates outcomes, not engagement.
